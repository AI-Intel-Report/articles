# Contributing to the AI Intel Report Open Archive

Thank you for your interest in engaging with [AI Intel Report's](https://aiintelreport.com) open knowledge archive. This is a living record of our journalism and analysis, and the community has a genuine role to play in keeping it accurate, well-sourced, and useful.

---

## How You Can Contribute

### Corrections

Accuracy is our highest obligation. If you find a factual error in any article in this archive — a misattributed finding, an incorrect date, a technical inaccuracy, a misquoted source — please open an issue with:

1. The file path of the article in question
2. The specific text that is incorrect (quote it directly)
3. The correct information, with a source or reference if available

We review correction reports promptly. Confirmed corrections are applied to the archive and noted in the article's change history. The canonical version at [aiintelreport.com](https://aiintelreport.com) is updated in parallel.

We take corrections seriously and do not treat them as adversarial. If you have found something wrong, you are doing us a service.

### Clarification Requests

If an article is unclear, ambiguous, or could be made more precise without changing its substance, open an issue describing:

1. The file and the specific passage
2. What you found unclear or ambiguous
3. What additional context or precision would help

We distinguish between clarifications (the information is correct but could be expressed more clearly) and corrections (the information is wrong). Both are welcome.

### Citation Suggestions

If our coverage omits a significant primary source, a relevant research paper, a public dataset, or a disclosure that would materially affect how a piece should be read, open an issue with the reference details and a brief note on its relevance.

We do not guarantee inclusion of every suggested citation, but we review all suggestions and incorporate relevant ones.

### Reuse Notifications

If you have reused or adapted content from this archive in your own work — a research paper, a presentation, an article, a dataset, or a software project — we would love to know. Open an issue or send a brief note. We maintain no formal registry, but we are genuinely interested in how our work travels and what it helps people build.

This is also a good way to flag how you cited us, so we can confirm the attribution meets CC BY 4.0 requirements.

---

## What We Are Not Accepting

To be direct about scope:

- **Unsolicited article submissions** — we do not accept guest posts or contributed articles through this repository. Our editorial process is internal.
- **Pull requests that change article content** — editorial content is owned and reviewed by the AI Intel Report team. We will not merge PRs that modify article text, even well-intentioned ones. Submit a correction issue instead.
- **Promotional content, link insertions, or sponsored additions** — any contribution designed to promote a product, service, or external interest will be closed without review.
- **Speculation, opinion, or interpretation disputes** — we welcome factual corrections; we do not adjudicate editorial judgment calls through the issue tracker.

---

## Before You Open an Issue

- **Search existing issues** to see if your concern has already been raised.
- **Be specific.** Vague reports are hard to act on. Quote the exact text, provide the file path, include your source.
- **Be civil.** We work hard to get things right and take corrections seriously. The tone of engagement here should reflect the publication's standards.

---

## How We Handle Contributions

All issues are reviewed by the AI Intel Report editorial team. We aim to acknowledge reports within a reasonable timeframe and resolve confirmed corrections promptly.

We do not maintain a public contributor list or offer compensation for contributions. What we do offer is a fast, transparent, and good-faith review of every legitimate report.
